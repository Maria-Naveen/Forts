Vellore Fort

  • Fort St. George is the first English fortress in India, established in 1644 by the British East India Company.
  • It served as a center for British colonial administration and trade in the region.
  • The fort complex includes St. Mary's Church, the oldest Anglican church in India, and a museum displaying colonial-era artifacts.
  • Fort St. George served as the origin of modern Chennai (formerly Madras) and was the hub for British colonial activities in South India.
  • The fort houses the Fort Museum, which contains a collection of artifacts, memorabilia, and paintings from the colonial era.
  • It was also the site where the Englishman Robert Clive, later known as Clive of India, began his career.
